<p><p>In-situ Hybridization (ISH) is a molecular diagnostic technique used to detect specific nucleic acid sequences within tissue samples. The market is primarily segmented into two types: Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH) and Chromogenic In Situ Hybridization. FISH utilizes fluorescent probes for real-time imaging and precise identification of genetic anomalies, making it suitable for various applications, including cancer diagnostics. Chromogenic In Situ Hybridization employs enzyme-linked detection methods, providing a colorimetric response for visualization under a standard microscope, commonly used in pathology and research.</p></p>
