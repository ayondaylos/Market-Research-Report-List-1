<p><p>The DC step drives market encompasses two primary motor types: brushed and brushless motors. Brushed motors, characterized by their simplicity and cost-effectiveness, utilize mechanical brushes to transfer current to the rotor, making them easy to control but less efficient and shorter in lifespan. In contrast, brushless motors offer higher efficiency, better torque-to-weight ratios, and reduced maintenance due to the absence of brushes, making them ideal for applications requiring precision and longevity. Both types serve diverse industries, including automotive and robotics.</p></p>
