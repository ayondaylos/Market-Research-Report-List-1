<p><p>The InGaAs camera market comprises two main types: cooled and uncooled cameras. Cooled InGaAs cameras utilize thermoelectric or cryogenic cooling to reduce thermal noise, enabling high-performance imaging in low-light and infrared applications. They are ideal for scientific research, surveillance, and industrial applications. Uncooled InGaAs cameras, on the other hand, operate at ambient temperatures, offering a more compact and cost-effective solution for applications like thermal imaging and basic spectroscopy. Both types cater to distinct needs based on performance and budget.</p></p>
