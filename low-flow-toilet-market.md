<p><p>The low-flow toilet market encompasses various types, primarily siphonic and wash-down toilets. Siphonic toilets use a siphoning action to efficiently clear waste with minimal water, making them highly effective in water conservation. In contrast, wash-down toilets rely on gravity to flush waste into the trapway, typically requiring more water but offering fewer clogs. Both types are designed to reduce water usage compared to traditional toilets, catering to environmental concerns and regulatory standards for sustainable water management.</p></p>
